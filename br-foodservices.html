The Problem / El Problema

Markdown Problem statement

B&R Food Services, a food service supply distribution company, received an invoice from vendor Wellcare International for $7,133 against a purchase order of $10,527. The gap of $3,393 was not self-explanatory.

Without a structured model, the AP team would need to manually review 46 line items with no traceability, no risk classification, and no documented process. The real risk: approving a payment with errors, generating ERP inconsistencies, and having zero visibility into vendor error patterns over time.

Sin un modelo estructurado, el equipo de Cuentas por Pagar tendría que revisar 46 líneas manualmente, sin trazabilidad, sin clasificación de riesgo y sin proceso documentado. Riesgo real: aprobar un pago incorrecto y no detectar patrones de error por proveedor.

Analytical Decisions / Decisiones de Criterio

Markdown Human judgment over automation · Criterio humano sobre automatización
Decision 01 · Decisión 01
Classify into 4 categories, not just flag differences
Most basic models only flag whether a difference exists. I classified each discrepancy into a specific category because each one requires a different corrective action: quantity mismatch → credit memo; price mismatch → renegotiation; unauthorized charge → rejection; missing delivery → follow-up. Without this distinction, the AP team does not know what to do with each line.
Decision 02 · Decisión 02
Separate real errors from authorized adjustments
Item WC-HC66W showed a higher quantity than ordered. An automated model would have flagged it as an error. I documented that the extra quantity was internally approved by management, preventing a false positive that could have triggered an unnecessary vendor dispute.
Decision 03 · Decisión 03
Question prices that make no operational sense
Knife WCKE305W appeared on the invoice at $51.50 when the agreed PO price was $11.00. A 4.5x difference on a basic consumable is not a price change — it is a vendor data entry error. That distinction changes the action entirely: not renegotiation, but immediate correction before releasing payment.
Decision 04 · Decisión 04
Design for adoption, not to impress
I chose Excel / Google Sheets because the AP team already operates in that environment. A Python or SQL model would have required intermediaries to run it every cycle. The tool needs to live where the problem lives — immediate adoption, zero learning curve.

Business Recommendations / Recomendaciones al Negocio

Recommendations Actionable — Accionables
01

Do not release payment until critical discrepancies are resolved

The 2 items billed without a PO and the 3 with quantity errors represent charges that should not be paid without a vendor credit memo. No liberar el pago hasta obtener nota de crédito.

02

Confirm whether WC-PW83 was substituted by WC-PW93

The vendor invoiced a different SKU than what was ordered. Without written confirmation, payment for that item remains in dispute. Confirmar sustitución por escrito antes de pagar.

03

Implement this model as the standard AP control

Manual line-by-line review every cycle is not scalable. This model automates the reconciliation: paste the ERP export, the cross-reference runs automatically, and follow-up is documented in the same file. Estandarizar como control previo al pago en cada ciclo de AP.

04

Use the history log to audit vendor error patterns over time

The accumulated history enables detection of whether a vendor systematically makes errors in price or quantity — high-value intelligence for future negotiations. Usar el historial para detectar patrones y negociar condiciones.
